Proper Posture: Sitting Guide

Step 1: Sit with your back straight and shoulders pulled slightly backward.
Step 2: Keep your feet flat on the floor or on a footrest.
Step 3: Adjust your chair height so your knees are at a 90-degree angle.
Step 4: Place your hips as far back in the chair as possible, aligning your spine with the backrest.
Step 5: Use a cushion or lumbar support for lower back support.
Step 6: Avoid slouching or leaning forward for extended periods.

Injury Prevention Tips

General Tips

Warm up before any physical activity (5-10 minutes of dynamic stretching).
Use proper equipment, like supportive footwear or braces.
Maintain proper technique during exercises and sports.
Avoid overtraining by scheduling rest days.

For Runners

Gradually increase mileage by no more than 10% per week.
Run on even surfaces to avoid ankle injuries.

For Weightlifters

Always use proper form and avoid lifting too much weight.
Engage your core during lifts to protect your spine.

For Office Workers

Adjust your workstation to avoid back and neck strain.
Take short breaks every 30 minutes to stretch and move.

Best Practices for Recovery

  • Follow medical advice and stick to prescribed physical therapy plans.
  • Avoid pushing through pain or rushing recovery.
  • Prioritize proper nutrition for healing (e.g., protein, vitamin C, calcium).
  • Stay consistent with exercises and monitor progress.

Post-Surgery Care

  • Wound Care: Keep the surgical area clean and dry.
  • Pain Management: Use prescribed medications and non-invasive techniques like ice or elevation.
  • Physiotherapy: Attend regular sessions to regain mobility.
  • Prevent Complications: Watch for signs of infection, blood clots, or excessive swelling.

First Aid For Orthopedic injuries

Dislocated joints

Symptoms of Dislocation

  • Severe pain and inability to move the joint.
  • Visible deformity or abnormal positioning of the joint.
  • Swelling and bruising.

Dislocated Joint - First-Aid Steps

Dislocations are serious injuries that require prompt medical attention.

  1. Do not attempt to relocate the joint. Trying to do so can cause more damage.
  2. Stabilize the joint by immobilizing it with a splint or sling.
  3. Apply ice to reduce swelling and numb the pain. Use a cloth to wrap the ice to avoid frostbite.
  4. Get medical help immediately. Dislocations often require professional medical intervention to properly reset the joint.

Sprained Ankle

Symptoms of a Sprained Ankle

  • Swelling around the ankle joint.
  • Bruising.
  • Pain when bearing weight or moving the ankle.
  • Limited range of motion.

Sprained Ankle - First-Aid Steps

A sprained ankle involves overstretched ligaments around the ankle joint. Here's how to help:

  1. Rest the ankle and avoid putting weight on it. Elevate the leg when possible.
  2. Ice the area to reduce swelling. Apply for 15-20 minutes every 1-2 hours during the first 48 hours.
  3. Compression with an elastic bandage can help control swelling, but avoid wrapping it too tightly.
  4. Elevation above the level of the heart is crucial for reducing swelling.
  5. If the ankle is extremely painful or swollen, seek medical attention immediately.

Fractures

Symptoms of a Fracture

  • Severe pain.
  • Swelling and bruising.
  • Deformity or an unnatural position of the limb.
  • Inability to move or use the injured part.

Fracture - First-Aid Steps

Fractures are serious and require immediate attention. Follow these steps:

  1. Do not attempt to move the injured limb. If possible, keep the patient still.
  2. Immobilize the fracture using a splint, stick, or any rigid material. Ensure the limb is held in place.
  3. Apply ice to the injury site to reduce swelling and numb the pain.
  4. If there’s bleeding, apply gentle pressure to control it with a clean cloth or bandage.
  5. Seek medical help immediately to properly realign and treat the fracture.

Soft Tissue Injuries (Strains, Sprains, Contusions)

Symptoms of Soft Tissue Injuries

  • Pain at the injury site.
  • Swelling and bruising.
  • Difficulty moving the injured area.

Soft Tissue Injuries - First-Aid Steps

Soft tissue injuries such as strains and sprains can be painful but manageable with the right care:

  1. Rest the injured area to avoid further strain.
  2. Ice the injury to control swelling and alleviate pain.
  3. Compression with an elastic bandage can help reduce swelling.
  4. Elevation above the heart will help decrease swelling.
  5. If symptoms persist or worsen, seek medical attention for further evaluation.
